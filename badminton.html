BADMINTON

Badminton is a popular racquet sport played using a shuttlecock and a racquet. It is usually played as singles or doubles on a rectangular court divided by a net. The fast-paced nature of the game makes it exciting and challenging.

The sport has its roots in ancient games played in India and China, but modern badminton was developed in England during the 19th century. Today, it is played worldwide and is especially popular in Asian countries like India, China, and Indonesia.

Players score points by hitting the shuttlecock over the net in such a way that the opponent cannot return it. Agility, reflexes, and stamina are key to success in badminton, making it both a physical and strategic game.
