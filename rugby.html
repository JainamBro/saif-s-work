RUGBY

Rugby is a dynamic and physical team sport that originated in England during the 19th century. It is played with an oval-shaped ball and is known for its combination of strength, strategy, and teamwork.

The game is divided into two main forms: Rugby Union and Rugby League, each with slightly different rules. Despite these variations, both emphasize tackling, passing, and scoring tries by carrying the ball over the opponentâ€™s goal line.

Rugby is particularly popular in countries like New Zealand, South Africa, England, and Australia. The Rugby World Cup is one of the biggest international tournaments, attracting millions of fans worldwide.

The sport promotes values of respect, discipline, and camaraderie, making it not only a competitive game but also a symbol of sportsmanship and unity among nations.
